Mircea F. Lungu, Ph.D.

I am a researcher at the Software Composition Group at the University of Berne. I work closely with Oscar Nierstrasz and the members of the group.

I got my PhD from the University of Lugano, with Michele Lanza as advisor and my engineer degree from the Polytechnic University of Timişoara, with a thesis supervised by Radu Marinescu and Tudor Girba. I was as a visiting researcher at IBM TJ Watson Research Center in NY with Wim De Pauw.

My research interests are in software engineering, software evolution, ecosystem analysis, programming languages, and mobile applications.

Research

Software Evolution
Agile Software Assessment - SNF project investigating software evolution
SATToSE (new) - We are organizing it at the University of Bern. Consider attending!
Softwarenaut - Tool for monitoring software architecture evolution.
Pangea - Making multi-language empirical studies in software engineering easy
Big Software Data - Empirical Studies of large scale software analysis
Software Ecosystems
St1PL1 - A Toolchain for PL1 Ecosystem analysis developed in collaboration with Credit Suisse (not public yet)
WEA (new) - We are organizing the First International Workshop on Ecosystem Architectures in Saint Petersburg (co-located with ESEC/FSE)
Languages
Polite - Investigating PL syntaxes closer to natural language
Zee Guu - Ubiquitous second language acquisition
Visualization
Quicksilver - Infrastructure for the interactive exploration of hierarchical data sets

A good part of the software analysis work goes into Moose to which I and my students have been contributing since several years.

Teaching

I teach since a long time. In my 2nd year of bachelor I was already teaching the introductory programming course at a technical highschool in Timisoara, Romania. I had one student who was a prison warden during the day. Teaching him to code was awesome. Teaching is learning, so I have been lucky to be teaching five courses until now:

  1. Compiler Construction,
  2. Concurrency Patterns,
  3. Software Design and Evolution,
  4. Principles of UI Design,
  5. Human Computer Interaction.

In the fall of 2013 I will be teaching the Software Engineering course at the University of Bern.

Service to the Community

I served on the PC of: OOPSLA, ICSE TD Track, WCRE, ICSM TD Track, WASDeTT. For a full list see my resume.

Three of the Swiss and international events that I organize are:

  1. The International Workshop on Ecosystem Architectures co-located in 2013 with ESEC/FSE.
  2. The Seminar on Advanced Tools and Techniques for SE at the University of Bern in July 2013.
  3. The CHOOSE Forum 2012: Models vs. Services

I review for the following periodicals:
